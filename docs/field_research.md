## Individual Research: [Your Full Name]

### Observation Notes
- **Date:** March 26, 2026
- **Time:** [Add time of observation, e.g., 2:30 PM]
- **Location:** Campus

**Findings:**
- Students post items for sale on WhatsApp status and groups
- Common items: clothes, shoes, books, electronics
- Negotiations happen via WhatsApp chat
- Delivery arrangements are informal (meetups on campus or hostels)
- Posts get lost in chat history with no search functionality
- No way to verify if product images are real or stock photos
- Prices are often changed when buyer shows interest

### User Interview

**Interviewee:** ASU  
**Year:** Year 3 Student  
**Date:** March 26, 2026  
**Duration:** ~6 minutes

**Interview Questions & Responses:**

| Question | Response |
|----------|---------|
| Have you ever bought or sold items from other students? | "Currently I have not. But I have a friend of mine who did such kind of a thing like he normally sells some kind of stuff, some clothes or what to his friends." |
| What problems did you face when trying to buy or sell on campus? | "The problem is that when there is a time when people like our colleagues from campus here, they post like those products on their WhatsApp status in groups... when you approach him, why not buy it? And he's going to increase the price... you posted this and you said it's 10k. Now you tell me it's 15k. Why?" |
| How do you currently find items you need? | "They post like shoes on their status and if you find one interesting or attractive you should be like can I buy this one? And you negotiate via WhatsApp chats and then after you prepare a meetup or there be a delivery." |
| What would make it easier for you to buy and sell on campus? | "If there's a website or an application where each student in this campus can go directly search the product he wants... whether it's cloth, shoe or other products... and then if there's that website like we know it, it has been published then you can go there and search your product and buy from there and there'll be some deliveries like in the hostel or where we live." |
| Have you ever had a bad experience with buying or selling? | "One very bad... He can post a product and take a very good picture from like Google or ChatGPT... and posts that picture like so attractive... when you approach him or her to buy and you find that product very uninteresting... it's used, it's not brand new." |
